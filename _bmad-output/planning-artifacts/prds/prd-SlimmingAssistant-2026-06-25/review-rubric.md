# PRD Quality Review — SlimmingAssistant

## Overall verdict

The PRD is decision-ready for UX, architecture, and story decomposition at hobby/solo-project rigor. The scope is explicit, the feature requirements are testable, and the product thesis is coherent: record personal health/running data, expose goal progress, and reduce abandonment through reminders.

## Decision-readiness — strong

The PRD states the key product choices directly: personal-only use, manual entry, Web first, no third-party import, no account system, and browser-based trusted-device access protection. Open questions are cleared.

## Substance over theater — strong

Sections are earned by the product shape. User journeys are lightweight and tied to FRs. NFRs are product-specific rather than generic.

## Strategic coherence — strong

The features serve a single loop: set goals, record data, inspect progress, receive reminders, continue action. Success metrics validate that loop instead of optimizing for feature count.

## Done-ness clarity — adequate

FRs include testable outcomes. The only area that may need architecture-level elaboration is the exact storage and invalidation mechanism for trusted device identifiers, which is appropriately left for architecture.

## Scope honesty — strong

Non-goals and MVP boundaries are explicit. Deferred integrations and medical/AI capabilities are clearly out of scope.

## Downstream usability — strong

FR, UJ, and SM IDs are stable and contiguous. Glossary terms are defined for downstream UX, architecture, and story creation.

## Shape fit — strong

The document is appropriately lightweight for a solo personal Web app while still specific enough to feed the BMad downstream planning sequence.
